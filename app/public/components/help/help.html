Frequently Asked Questions

    What is Local Learners?

    Local Learners is a free to join group dedicated to providing new experiences for people. We allow anyone who has something to teach a way to do so. We try to help find teachers for heavily requested topics. And we try to find facilities for teachers to use for their meet ups.

    Are the teachers any good?

    We've never had any complaints, and that's a pretty good track record in our books. You should know going in that we allow ANYONE to teach. This means anyone from amatuers and hobbyists to professionals and world class experts could host. Teachers typically provide a little back story of their experience when hosting an event. This should give you a good idea of what to expect.

    Plus almost all lessons are free, so you're bound to learn something new, even from a noobie teacher. Just remember that it's all about having fun and trying something new, not getting it perfect your first try.

    Do I have to pay to take a class?

    All of our classes are free!

    We want the barrier of entry to be low to make sure anyone who wants to learn, can. So classes are always free. However...

    • We do allow for teachers to charge to cover the cost of materials. This isn't the same as requiring someone to bring your own.
    • For some classes, there is an obvious cost incurred on the learner, such as a cooking class. You would be expected to purchase and bring your own ingredients. So in that case, it costs you money for the ingredients but not the class.
    • In other scenarios the teacher may provide the materials and have you pay when you show up. For example, a class on how to use a pottery wheel may charge $5 when you arrive to cover the cost of the clay you'll be using that evening.
    How do I know if I am the right skill level for a class?

    The pre-requisites section of a class should give you an indication of what level you should be at. If you're not sure, just send the teacher a message on Meetup.

    Do I need to bring anything to class?

    Check the prerequisites section of the meetup to see if any materials are required.
